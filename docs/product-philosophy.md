# Product Philosophy

## Product test

1. **Loved tool:** What existing behavior or quality are we preserving?
2. **Real friction:** What repeated user problem have we observed?
3. **Material improvement:** Can a person perceive the benefit in normal use?
4. **Responsible intelligence:** Is AI appropriate, understandable, and overridable?
5. **Long relationship:** Can the product be maintained, supported, and trusted?

## Tradeoff order

Safety and honest claims; user agency and core experience; reliability and manufacturability; repairability and lifecycle; performance; cost; feature breadth; novelty. Cost is real, but cannot silently override safety or trust.

## AI boundary

AI output is design input, not authority. Every AI-influenced requirement or release claim needs a named human owner, traceable evidence, and explicit review.
